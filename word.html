Global catastrophes have occurred many times in history, even if we only count disasters causing more than 10 million deaths.

Introduction

Throughout history, there have been multiple occasions that have threatened much of humanity. These occasions are termed as 'Global Catastrophic Risks' and some might even have the ability to permanently and drastically curtail humanity's potential. There are a multitude of such 'Global Catastrophic Risks' that can occur within our lifetime.

However, it doesn't mean we can't defend ourselves from these or prevent these from ever happening. In fact, there have already been measures taken to ensure that these catastrophes don't occur or have a minimal impact. We will go through some of these risks and talk about what has been done,or what can be done to prevent/reduce the impact of such events.

Pandemics

In the past 2 years, every single nation on this planet has been impacted by COVID-19, and it has killed approximately 4.44 million people globably, and the number would continue to rise. However, this is nothing compared to past global catastrophes. As a percentage of population, COVID-19 has only killed around 0.0563% of population, which is nothing compared to the impact of the Black Plague, which wiped out around 30%-40% of the population which it affected.

Image by wikimedia.com
A pandemic is declared when a disease spreads across countries and continents, having more infectivity and lethality than a regular disease. In the modern era of yper-connectivity, and faster travel times, it's easier than ever before for such diseases to spread. However, the disease needs to be contagious to be declared a pandemic. Eg: Cancer is not declared a pandemic because it is unable to transfer from one person to another.

Pandemics often lead to economic unstability, and a disruption in social wellbeing, brought in by the nature of the disease.

Prevention and Solutions

Isolation

Image by NYT

By isoltating the infected individuals, and ensuring that people are social distancing uto prevent further sperad of the disease, a community can aim to "flatten the curve". The term flatten the curve refers to decreasing the peak of the pandemic.

This is done to not put too much burden on the healthcare system by overwhelming them with new cases, but rather having the cases be spread out over a longer period of time,

u> ensuring that addequete supplies and care is available for infected individuals.

Isolating and social distancing practices also help in breaking the chain of transmissions between an infected individual and a healthy person, shortening the length of the pandemic.

Vaccination

Image by https://reuters.com

One of the most effective ways to stop a pandemic is through vaccines. Vaccines provide the body with aweakened version of the disease so the body can learn to fight the diseas, and be prepared to fight the actual disease.

However, some people might not be able to get vaccines due to a weakened immune system, or due to other medical condition(s). To ensure that these individuals don't get the disease, it is important that a community develops herd immunity. Herd immunity is developed when 60-70% of a community has gained immunity (in our case, through vaccines), virtually eleminating the disease from the community and ensuring that those who cannot get vaccines are not impacted by the disease.

Cosmic Threats

Cosmic threats are some of the few catastrophes that humans have no direct control over. These cosmic events include, but are not limited to asteroid impacts, close encounters with a black hole, gamma ray bursts, etc. However, not all of these are equally likely.

An asteroid impact is the most likely cosmic threat. An asteroid bigger than 1-2km can be disastrous for the the planet and all species.


Image by gaia.com

Threats such as gamma ray bursts are highly unlikely to naturally point in the direction of our planet. However, if such a scenario plays out, the strong burst of energy will most likely strip off Earth's ozone layeru>, completly destroying most life on the planet.

A likely cosmic threat that can occur anytime is a powerful solar flare, where areas of sun suddenly increase in brightness, often ejecting highly energetic particles, and coronal masses. If these hit Earth, they can trigger geomagnetic storms that can disable satelites, and cause massive electrical shutdowns all over the world, and with most of our population relying on electricty in their day to day life, it will have drastic impact on them, especially the healthcare sector.


Image by wired.com

Prevention and Solutions

Research

Image by https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net

Since humans are not responsible for any cosmic event, and these event can happen at any random time, it is extremely hard to prepare for such events.

However, for events such as asteroid impacts, the best bet is to increase the fundings of research agencies such as NASA, so that they have more resources to look into the night sky and search for any potential asteroid that might be on course towards our planet.

One suggested method to prevent an asteroid impact after detection has been "Kinetic Impact".u> This means hitting the asteroid with a massive object such as a rocket, or possibly any near Earth, to deter the asteroid from its course.

Climate Change


Image by guim.co.uk
Climate change is one of the few catastrophes which have a realistic chance of endangering much of human existence. Industrial revolution and it's consequences have been disastrous for the human race. It has led to an unpresedented increase in Carbon in the atmosphere, which has the ability to trap sunlight and warm up the atmosphere.

To buffer this excessive amount of carbon in the atmosphere, theocean starts absorbing the carbon, heating it up as well, resulting in the melting of the ice caps. Ice caps reflect most of the solar heat back, but ocean water absorbs most of this heat. This leads to a feedback loop where the melting up of ice leads to oceans heating up faster, which further melts the ice caps.


Image by theguardian.com
Climate change has already started affecting humans. Coastal cities around the world are already starting to flood every year, and every year is becomming the hottest on record. This makes it even harder to grow suitable crops at the right time. Climate Change has already been linked to approximately 5 million deaths every year, and the number is only set to drastically increase unless something is done about Climate change.

Prevention and Solutions

Reduce carbon emissions

Image by gstatic

Reducing carbon emissions is one of the most effective ways to stop global warming. By reducing carbon emissions, the amount of carbon in the atmosphere will reduce. This will reduce the rate of warming up of the planet.

Taking steps as having a more plant based diet, better farming techniques, and building more renewable energy infrastructure can aid in the process of reaching net zero carbon emissions.

Creating better cities

Image by https://timeout.com

An effective way to combat climate change is by creating better cities. Around 56% of the world lives in cities and this number is only going to go higher. Therefore, it is essential that cities help in creating a better life for its citizens.

Ensuring good public transport infrastructure is a key component in creating a better city. This ensures that people don't need to use cars to travel everywhere when they have better alternative method of travelling. Creating more walkable, and accesible paths is also important to create a better city, along with planting plenty of trees, and ensuring people can access basic amenities at a walking distance.

Warfare

In the year 1983, the world almost came close to its end. The Soviet Union nuclear detectors detected a bunch of intercontinental ballistic missiles, from the bases in the United States. However, the officer of the Soviet Air Defence Forces on duty concluded that these were false alarms, which they were, and therefore none of the "missiles" ever hit the USSR.

Had he thought those were actual "missiles" and retalitated by attacking the United States, it would have led to an all-out nuclear war.


Image by sipri.org

Image by wikimedia
There are currently over 15,700 nuclear weapons worldwide. Nukes can not only cause direct death, which can range around a couple hundred thousand to millions, but indirect too. It is estimated that only 100 nukes are required to send the planet into a nuclear winter, where the dust from fallout is more than enough to cover the Sun.

This can cause the planet to be unable to grow crops properly for years, leading to mass starvation, and also cause the temperatures to drop drastically, making the living conditions harder.

Prevention and Solutions

Denuclearization

Denuclearization is one of the safest best we can make against nuclear and warfare threat. This means that no country would be allowed to have any nukes and they would need to decomission their existing nukes. Therefore, even if countries go in wars, it is safe to assume that they would not have the cabalities to destroy the entirety of the planet.

Global Peace

Global peace is another way we can assure that there is no threat of nuclear warfare and extinction on humanity. If there is no more conflict between countries, there will be no reason for them to go to war with each other, and therefore no reason for them to use their nuclear weapons.

The United Nations is one such organisation that is trying to achieve this goal. It was formed for the sole purpose of ensuring peace between nations, and it has worked incredibly well. There has been no major war since its formation, only minor conflicts here and there, and that too are decreasing at a fast pace.
